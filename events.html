Eid al-Fitr

Muslims give charity (Zakat al-Fitr) the night before Eid. On the day of Eid, they perform a special prayer (Salat al-Eid). Celebrations include visiting friends and family, sharing meals, and giving gifts..

Eid al-Adha

Muslims perform Qurbani, sacrificing an animal. Special prayer (Salat al-Eid) is offered. Meat from the sacrifice is shared with others, and the day involves feasting and charity..

Hajj

Pilgrims enter a consecrated state (Ihram) before reaching Mecca. Perform rituals, including Tawaf, walking around the Kaaba, and traveling between Safa and Marwah. Stand on the plain of Arafat and perform symbolic acts like Stoning the Devil..

Islamic New Year (Hijri New Year)

The Islamic calendar begins, marking the migration of Prophet Muhammad from Mecca to Medina (Hijra). Muslims may reflect on the past year, seek forgiveness, and make resolutions for the new year..

Mawlid al-Nabi (Prophet Muhammad's Birthday)

Celebrates the birth of Prophet Muhammad. Muslims may attend special prayers, listen to sermons, and participate in processions. Some communities engage in charitable activities and share stories about the life of the Prophet..

Laylat al-Qadr (Night of Power)

Falls within the last ten nights of Ramadan. Muslims engage in intense prayers and supplications, seeking the night when the Quran was first revealed. It is a time for self-reflection, seeking forgiveness, and asking for blessings..

Ashura

Commemorates various historical events, including the day Allah saved Prophet Musa (Moses) and the Israelites. For Shia Muslims, it also marks the martyrdom of Imam Hussein. Practices may include fasting, mourning rituals, and charitable acts.

Mi'raj (Night Journey and Ascension)

Commemorates the night when Prophet Muhammad was taken from Mecca to Jerusalem and ascended to the heavens. Muslims may engage in special prayers and reflections on the significance of the event..

Lailatul Baraat (Night of Forgiveness)

This night, observed during the month of Shaban, is considered a night of forgiveness and mercy. Muslims engage in special prayers and seek forgiveness for their sins. It is a time for reflection, repentance, and seeking Allah's mercy..

Day of Jumu'ah (Friday)

While not a specific event, Fridays hold special significance in Islam. Muslims gather for the Jumu'ah prayer, a congregational prayer that includes a sermon. It is a day for increased acts of worship and supplication..
