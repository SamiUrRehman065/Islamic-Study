Introduction to Sawm

Sawm, or fasting, constitutes the fourth pillar of Islam and is observed by Muslims during the month of Ramadan. This sacred month holds special significance as it marks the period during which the first verses of the Quran were revealed to Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). Fasting during Ramadan is a rigorous and holistic act of worship that extends beyond abstaining from food and drink. It encompasses refraining from negative behaviors such as gossip, anger, and impatience. Muslims engage in a pre-dawn meal, Suhoor, and conclude their fast with Iftar at sunset. The act of fasting serves as a spiritual and physical discipline, fostering self-control, empathy for the less fortunate, and a heightened awareness of God's presence. Additionally, it offers an opportunity for increased prayer, reflection, and acts of charity. The end of Ramadan is celebrated with the joyous festival of Eid al-Fitr, during which Muslims express gratitude to Allah for the strength and perseverance demonstrated during the month of fasting. In essence, Sawm is a deeply transformative and communal experience that strengthens the individual's connection with Allah and reinforces a sense of shared responsibility and empathy within the global Muslim community..

Hadiths on Sawm

On the Spiritual Rewards of Fasting:

Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: "Whoever fasts during Ramadan with faith and seeking his reward from Allah will have his past sins forgiven." (Sahih al-Bukhari).

On the Essence of Fasting:

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "When any one of you is observing Saum (fasting) on a day, he should neither indulge in obscene language nor should he raise his voice; and if anyone reviles him or tries to quarrel with him, he should say: 'I am observing fast.'" (Sahih Muslim).

On the Night of Power (Laylat al-Qadr):

Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: "Whoever prays during the night of Qadr with faith and seeking his reward from Allah will have his past sins forgiven." (Sahih al-Bukhari).

On the Reward for Breaking the Fast:

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "He who gives water to drink to a fasting person will receive a reward from Allah, and he who gives milk to a fasting person will receive a reward from Allah, and Allah will admit (him) to Paradise." (Sunan Ibn Majah).

On the Joy of Breaking the Fast:

Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: "The people will remain on the right path as long as they hasten the breaking of the fast." (Sahih al-Bukhari).

These Hadiths emphasize the spiritual rewards and forgiveness associated with fasting during Ramadan, the importance of maintaining good behavior and control of one's actions while fasting, the significance of Laylat al-Qadr (Night of Power), the rewards for providing sustenance to those fasting, and the recommended haste in breaking the fast. They collectively underscore the holistic nature of fasting and its profound impact on the individual's spiritual growth and connection with Allah.
